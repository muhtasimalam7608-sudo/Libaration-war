Oral Histories & Interviews

Listen to the voices of history. First-hand accounts from freedom fighters, survivors, and witnesses who lived through the 1971 Liberation War. Their stories preserve the truth for future generations.

Freedom Fighters Interviews

Bir Protik Karimullah

Mukti Bahini, Chittagong Hill Tracts

Awarded for bravery in the Hill Tracts region. Describes ambush tactics, coordination with local tribes, and liberation of key areas in the southeast.

Key Testimonies:

  • Guerrilla warfare tactics in difficult terrain
  • Relations with local indigenous communities
  • Capture of enemy outposts

Commander (Navy) Rezaul Karim

Naval Commando, Operation Jackpot

Participated in Operation Jackpot, the naval guerrilla campaign. Recounts mining enemy ships, daring raids on ports, and disrupting enemy supply lines.

Key Testimonies:

  • Naval commando training in India
  • Operation Jackpot details
  • Sabotage of enemy vessels

Civilian Experiences

Mrs. Rahima Begum

Refugee, Jessore

Fled her village with seven children, crossed the border to India. Describes the refugee camp conditions, loss of home, and eventual return after liberation.

Audio - 22 min

Mr. Shafiqul Islam

Student, Rajshahi

Participated in the student movement and later helped freedom fighters with intelligence gathering. Shares experiences of secret meetings and underground networks.

Video - 18 min

Mr. Nitai Chandra Das

Village Leader, Comilla

Organized shelter and food for displaced families. Coordinated with freedom fighters for village defense and helped hide civilians during military operations.

Audio - 35 min

Eyewitness Accounts

Journalist Kamal Lohani

Swadhin Bangla Betar Kendra

Worked at the clandestine radio station that broadcasted news and patriotic songs. Witnessed the cultural resistance movement and propaganda efforts against Pakistani forces.

Key Observations:

  • Operation of clandestine radio station
  • Impact of patriotic broadcasts on morale
  • Cultural resistance efforts

Father Joseph Gomes

Missionary, Dinajpur

Provided sanctuary to people of all faiths during military operations. Witnessed interfaith solidarity and describes how religious institutions became safe havens.

Key Observations:

  • Interfaith solidarity during crisis
  • Use of religious institutions as shelters
  • Humanitarian efforts across communities
